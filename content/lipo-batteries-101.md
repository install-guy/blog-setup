# LiPo Batteries 101: 3S vs 4S, Charging, Storage, and Safety

## Intro
LiPo batteries can completely change how your RC feels.

More punch. Better throttle response. A whole lot more fun when everything is dialed.

But this is also where a lot of people get into trouble, usually because nobody explained the basics in plain English.

---

## The Core Idea (In Plain English)
"S" just means how many cells are in the pack.

- 3S = 3 cells and more manageable power for a lot of everyday setups
- 4S = 4 cells and a bigger jump in speed, punch, and overall load

More cells means more voltage.
More voltage means more performance, but also more stress on the rest of the system.

That is why battery choice is never just about speed. It affects runtime, motor temperature, gearing tolerance, and how hard the whole car has to work.

---

## When This Matters (Use Cases)
### Scenario 1
- You are moving from NiMH or a mild 2S setup into something stronger
- Battery choice starts changing how the whole car behaves, not just how fast it goes

### Scenario 2
- You want more power but your setup already runs warm
- Higher voltage can turn a manageable heat issue into an expensive one fast

### Scenario 3
- Your packs seem to wear out quicker than they should
- Storage habits, charge settings, and general upkeep usually matter more than people think

---

## What to Look For / Key Considerations
- Match battery voltage to what your ESC and motor are rated for
- Stick to balance charging instead of rushing the process
- Store packs at proper storage voltage when they are not being used soon
- Watch for swelling, damaged wires, or packs that refuse to balance correctly
- Keep connectors clean and secure
- Check temperatures after changing voltage, gearing, or tire size
- Treat battery care as part of the same routine as your maintenance habits

---

## Common Mistakes
- Jumping to higher cell counts without checking temps
- Using the wrong charger setting for the battery type
- Leaving packs fully charged for long periods
- Running packs too low because the car still had a little left
- Treating puffed or damaged packs like they are still worth the risk

---

## Real Talk
Most LiPo problems are not bad luck. They are bad habits.

Charge correctly. Store properly. Match the battery to the setup instead of just chasing the biggest number on the label.

Do that, and LiPos are reliable, powerful, and absolutely worth it.
