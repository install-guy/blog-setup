# LiPo Batteries 101: 3S vs 4S, Charging, Storage, and Safety

## Intro
LiPo batteries can completely change how your RC feels.

More punch. Better throttle response. Way more fun when everything’s dialed.

But this is also where a lot of people get into trouble—usually because nobody explained the basics in plain English.

Let’s fix that.

---

## 3S vs 4S (What It Actually Means)
“S” just means how many cells are in the pack.

- **3S** = 3 cells (~11.1V)  
- **4S** = 4 cells (~14.8V)  

More cells = more voltage.  
More voltage = more speed, more punch… and more stress on your setup.

---

### When 3S makes sense
3S is the sweet spot for most people:
- Everyday bashing  
- Newer drivers  
- Lighter drivetrains  
- Longer component life  

It’s fast enough to be fun, but forgiving enough to stay in control.

---

### When 4S makes sense
4S is where things start getting serious:
- Higher top speed  
- Harder acceleration  
- More load on everything  

Great when your rig is built for it.

If your ESC or motor is only rated for 3S—don’t “just try” 4S. That’s how things get expensive fast.

## Charging (Keep It Simple and Safe)

A good battery routine should feel boring. That’s a good thing.

---

### Use a balance charger
Always run your packs in **balance mode**.

It keeps each cell in check instead of letting one drift and cause problems later.

---

### Start at 1C
Most people should stick to **1C charging**.

Example:
- 5000mAh battery → 5.0A charge rate  

Yes, some packs can charge faster. But 1C is easy, safe, and keeps batteries healthy longer.

---

### Don’t walk away while charging
Charge in a safe area. Use a LiPo bag if you have one.

And stay nearby.

This is not the time to “set it and forget it.”

## Storage and Safety (Where Problems Usually Start)

Most issues don’t happen while driving—they happen between runs.

---

### Storage voltage matters
If you’re not running soon, store your packs around **3.8V per cell**.

- Not fully charged  
- Not fully drained  

Both will shorten battery life.

---

### Watch for warning signs
Retire a pack if you see:
- Puffing or swelling  
- Damaged wires or connectors  
- Crushed or punctured case  
- Cells that won’t balance  

Not worth the risk.

---

### Common mistakes
- Running without low-voltage cutoff  
- Using the wrong charger setting  
- Sloppy adapters or loose connections  
- Jumping to higher cell counts without checking temps  
- Tossing packs loose where they can short  

These are all avoidable.
