# LiPo Storage and Safety

## Intro
Most LiPo problems do not start while you are driving.

They start on the bench, in the bag, or sitting too long fully charged because you meant to get to them later.

Good LiPo safety is mostly about simple habits, and those habits go a long way toward keeping your packs healthier and your routine a lot less stressful.

---

## The Core Idea (In Plain English)
LiPo storage and safety come down to treating the battery with a little respect between runs.

The biggest thing is storage voltage. If you are not using the pack soon, you do not want it sitting fully charged or fully drained. Storage voltage keeps the cells in a healthier range and helps the pack last longer.

Handling matters too. Damaged wires, puffing, crushed cases, and packs that will not balance properly are all warning signs. If you are still getting the basics locked in, [LiPo Batteries 101](/gearheads-gazette/lipo-batteries-101) is the right companion to this topic.

Safe charging, safe storage, and paying attention to pack condition are what keep LiPos reliable. Most battery trouble comes from skipping those boring steps, not from bad luck.

---

## When This Matters (Use Cases)
### Scenario 1
- You ran a few packs and are done for the day
- This is when proper storage habits matter more than anything else

### Scenario 2
- A pack looks a little swollen or the wires seem rough
- Catching those signs early is how you avoid bigger risk later

### Scenario 3
- You have batteries sitting for days or weeks between runs
- Long-term habits affect battery life just as much as hard driving does

---

## What to Look For / Key Considerations
- Store LiPos at storage voltage when they are not being used soon
- Charge in a safe area where you can actually keep an eye on the pack
- Check for puffing, wire damage, crushed corners, or packs that will not balance
- Keep packs out of excessive heat and away from loose metal objects
- Retire damaged packs instead of trying to squeeze one more run out of them
- Treat charging and storage as part of normal RC maintenance, not extra work

---

## Common Mistakes
- Leaving packs fully charged for too long
- Tossing batteries loose in a toolbox or gear bag
- Ignoring puffing because the pack still technically works
- Charging without paying attention to the settings
- Treating storage voltage like an optional step

---

## Real Talk
LiPo safety is not about being dramatic. It is about being consistent.

Most of the time, a safe battery routine is just a calm one. Charge them right, store them right, and stop trusting packs that are already showing signs of trouble.

The people who rarely have LiPo problems are usually the ones doing the basic stuff every single time.
