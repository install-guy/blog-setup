RC Maintenance Parts to Keep Your Rig Running Right

RC maintenance parts are the small pieces that keep a vehicle reliable run after run. Bearings, gears, hardware, shock parts, and driveline components may not be the flashiest upgrades, but they make a big difference. Staying ahead of wear keeps your rig smoother, quieter, cooler, and a lot less likely to fail in the middle of a good session.

Most maintenance problems start small. A rough bearing adds drag. A loose screw turns into damaged hardware. A worn gear gets noisy before it strips. Sloppy suspension parts make the whole car feel vague. If the vehicle sounds louder than normal, runs hotter, moves rough by hand, or has extra play in the drivetrain or suspension, something probably needs attention.

Preventative maintenance is not about tearing the whole car apart after every run. It is about checking the parts that take the most abuse and replacing worn pieces before they damage something more expensive. A consistent setup is easier to drive, easier to tune, and a lot more fun to own.

For most setups, it comes down to catching issues early before they turn into bigger failures.

  • Common parts to check: Bearings, spur gears, pinions, shock components, driveshafts, rod ends, hinge pins, and hardware.
  • Signs to watch for: Grinding noise, extra slop, heat, binding, leaking shocks, rough movement, or visible wear.
